Our Foundation

Founded two years ago, Asmita Marathi Natya Seva Sanstha was born out of a deep passion to preserve and promote the Marathi language among the new generation and the wider community. Realizing that Marathi art and theatre were gradually losing their audience, our founders created an organization dedicated to keeping the richness of our mother tongue alive through impactful acts, dramas, and performances.

Our Approach

We focus on theatre, workshops, stage performances, and street plays — in festivals, functions, and on-demand events — using every possible medium to make Marathi language meaningful and engaging, especially for children and young audiences. Our reach spans school children, college students, theatre enthusiasts, rural communities, and Marathi-speaking audiences everywhere, fostering a strong connection to culture and heritage.

Our Impact & Vision

In just two years, we have performed in multiple cities, welcomed new actors into our fold, and ensured that both participants and the organization benefit sustainably. Beyond performances, we actively contribute to social awareness, nurturing talent, and spreading the love for Marathi theatre far and wide. Our vision is to preserve Marathi theatre and culture, expanding our reach across India to touch the hearts of all native Marathi speakers, ensuring that the beauty of our language and arts continues to inspire generations to come.

Tribute To

Late Dr. Aparna Joshi

Former Head of Marathi Department, Madhav College, Ujjain

The spirit of Late Dr. Aparna Joshi remains the guiding soul of our mission, though her voice still echoes in every rehearsal and performance of the Asmita Marathi Natya Seva Sanstha. Dr. Joshi, our beloved Former Head of the Marathi Department at Madhav College, Ujjain, shared her heart with us; she loved us deeply, treating us not as students but as her own, and inspired in us the passion for the greater good of society. That affection, that unwavering belief she placed in our potential, is what compels us to fight for the survival of Marathi arts today. It is with a heavy heart we remember the profound tragedy that she passed away just before she could witness her first directed drama take the stage. That unfulfilled dream, a testament to her lifelong passion for theatre, is now a sacred trust. Every play we stage, every workshop we organize, is our solemn promise to her: we will ensure that the beautiful cultural legacy she cherished, and the curtain she was tragically unable to raise, will now thrive in the hearts of a new generation. We are who we are because of the indelible love and inspiration she left with us.
